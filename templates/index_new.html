Our Hypothesis
The most effective way to spread information is through trusted community members.


In the West Africa Ebola outbreak, cases began to decline when religious leaders and village chiefs started to educate people about social distancing and bringing the sick to Ebola treatment units.

How can we effectively and accurately identify trusted community members (influencers) within dense, urban settings?

Our SMS-based influencer identification mechanism utilizes the low-tech but ubiquitous access to basic cell service and involves all the relevant stakeholders - Public Health Officials, Community Influencers and the Local Community.
